Chapter History

A LEGACY
RENEWED

Chi Phi is the oldest social fraternity in the United States, founded at Princeton University on Christmas Eve, 1824. It was formed through the merger of three independent organizations — the Chi Phi Society, the Chi Phi Fraternity of the South, and the Secret Order of Chi Phi — united by the shared values of Truth, Honor, and Personal Integrity.

The Eta Delta Chapter was chartered at USC in 1934 as Chi Phi expanded westward. Through the decades it grew into one of the most diverse and academically strong houses on the Row, peaking at over 100 members with consistently top-tier GPAs.

In the late 1990s, leadership gaps from graduating upperclassmen led the chapter to leave the Row in 1999 — not for misconduct, but simply declining numbers. The alumni network, however, never disappeared. In Fall 2016, backed by an extraordinary show of alumni commitment, the IFC voted to welcome Chi Phi back to USC after a 17-year hiatus.

Today, over 1,000 Eta Delta alumni span business, government, medicine, and engineering — actively serving as career mentors, advisors, and alumni association officers driving the chapter forward.
